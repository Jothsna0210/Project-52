Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat
  3. Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensive tail that they use to wrap around the tree branches

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus
  3. Cicadas have three eyes in total

Panda

  1. An adult panda can eat up to 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  2. Pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  3. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit

Teddy Bear

  1. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906
  3. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff
